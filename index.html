Public Transport vs Bigger Roads

Why investing in buses, trains, and trams works better than expanding highways

The Problem Benefits Comparison

The Problem With Expanding Roads

Many cities try to fix traffic congestion by building more roads or widening existing ones. However, this often fails because of induced demand. When roads are expanded, more people start driving, which fills the new space quickly and congestion returns.

Road expansion also consumes large amounts of land, increases pollution, and encourages car dependency instead of sustainable transportation.

Benefits of Public Transport

Moves More People

A single train or tram can carry hundreds of passengers, reducing the number of cars needed on the road.

Less Traffic

When more people use transit, fewer cars are on the road, improving travel times for everyone.

Better for the Environment

Public transport produces far fewer emissions per passenger compared with cars.

Cheaper for Cities

Building rail or bus networks can move far more people using less space than massive highway projects.

Quick Comparison

Public Transport Investment

  • Moves large numbers of people
  • Lower emissions
  • Less land required
  • Supports walkable cities
  • Reduces traffic congestion

Road Expansion
